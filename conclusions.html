Conclusions

Findings and To-Dos: Economic Sustainability:

  • Finding: Blockchain offers significant growth potential in industries like finance, healthcare, and supply chain management. However, high implementation and operational costs (e.g., energy consumption) pose challenges.
  • To-Do: Invest in more energy-efficient consensus mechanisms (e.g., Proof-of-Stake) and incentivize training programs to bridge the skills gap for blockchain professionals.

Social Sustainability:

  • Finding: Blockchain improves transparency and access to financial systems, especially in underbanked regions, but public understanding and adoption remain low. Additionally, a lack of diverse representation in blockchain careers limits its inclusivity.
  • To-Do: Promote education and outreach programs to increase awareness, and create inclusive policies to encourage a broader range of participants in blockchain-related careers.

Environmental Sustainability:

  • Finding: Proof-of-Work blockchains have significant environmental impacts due to high energy consumption, contributing to carbon emissions. Emerging alternatives like Proof-of-Stake and carbon-neutral blockchains reduce this footprint.
  • To-Do: Transition to energy-efficient blockchain models and promote green energy initiatives within blockchain operations.

Environmental Sustainability

Blockchains can contribute to environmental sustainability by enabling more efficient resource management and tracking of supply chains, which can reduce waste and optimize energy use. Furthermore, innovative blockchain applications, such as carbon credit trading platforms, can support initiatives aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions and promoting renewable energy.
